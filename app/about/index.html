About the Project

The WPSP project is run by Professor Sana Haroon, Associate Professor History and Asian Studies, UMass Boston and Professor Sameen Mohsin Ali, Assistant Professor in the Mushtaq Gurmani School. Professor Haroon and Professor Mohsin Ali are founding members of WPSP and will be establishing a project Advisory Board in 2021-22.


Faculty Leads

Dr. Sana Haroon

Sana Haroon is Associate Professor of History and Asian Studies at UMass Boston and has a particular interest in the impact of government on society in modern South Asia. She authored Frontier of Faith (Columbia University Press, 2008) a study of the formation of the northwest frontier non-administered region of colonial India and The Mosques of Colonial South Asia: A Social and Legal History of Muslim Worship (I. B. Tauris, 2021). She teaches courses in oral history, colonial South Asia, Pakistan and Afghanistan and the Indian Ocean. Professor Haroon received her PhD at the School of Oriental and African Studies Department of South Asian History. She has held the Isobel Thornley Fellowship at SOAS, the Past and Present Foundation Postdoctoral Fellowship at the Institute of Historical Research and the Malathy Singh Lectureship at Yale University.

Dr. Sameen A. Mohsin Ali

Sameen A. Mohsin Ali is Assistant Professor of Political Science at the Mushtaq Gurmani School of Humanities and Social Sciences (MGSHSS). She completed her PhD in Politics and International Studies from SOAS University of London in 2018. Her doctoral work focused on bureaucratic politics and patterns of governance in Pakistan, with a particular focus on the education and irrigation bureaucracies in Punjab. Her wider research and teaching interests include state capacity, bureaucratic performance, and party politics in South Asia, public health governance, and the politics of donor engagement in LMICs. Her work has been published in World Development and Commonwealth and Comparative Politics. Dr Ali is a faculty lead at the Technology for People Initiative (TPI), a non-profit applied research centre based at LUMS, and a fellow of the Mahbub-ul-Haq Research Centre.

Privacy Policy

All participants in the project sign documents of consent to interview, record and transcribe and confer intellectual and other rights in the interview to the Project. Participants have the right to remain anonymous in their interviews and be identified by an identifying number only and to restrict access to the interviews for a number of years. Documentation of consent and gift of the interviews is maintained by the Advisory Board in a secure location.

Library/Archival Policy

Audio recordings and transcripts will be electronically catalogued, added to the LUMS library database, and will be made available strictly only on the library premises on a non-networked and password protected computer. The Advisory Board will review access protocols periodically to ensure equitable access to the archive. Archiving procedures will be established and documented in AY 2021-22.
